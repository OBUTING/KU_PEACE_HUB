The Portable Peace Dialogue Tree

A foldable, mostly-recycled tool that helps young people resolve small conflicts through dialogue instead of fighting — built for the 2026 Kenya Scouts National Theme: "Empowering Young People to Promote Dialogue and Peaceful Communities."

Why this project exists

Small conflicts need simple tools.

In many Kenyan schools and communities, small conflicts — bullying, misunderstandings, arguments — happen often because young people lack simple tools and skills for peaceful dialogue. The Peace Tree turns those conflicts into opportunities for understanding and friendship. It's made mostly from recycled materials, fully foldable for easy transport, and usable in schools, churches, and communities.

The parts

How the tree looks

Height 90–100 cm assembled; folds flat to 5–10 cm thick, like a big book, for easy carrying. Built from recycled cardboard, plastic bottles, strings, old magazines, and bottle caps.

Foldable trunk & base

A brown cardboard trunk that folds book-style, with a foldable base and slot-in branches.

8 labeled leaves

Respect, Listen, Forgive, Understand, Unite, Empathise, Share, Peace — one value per leaf.

Spinning Peace Wheel

Conflict → Talk → Understand → Peace. Try it below.

Message cylinder

A recycled plastic bottle where anyone can drop a written message.

Peace Promise Chain

A paper chain of promises, growing link by link as people commit.

Stories & scorecard

Real conflict-story cards plus a Before & After Scorecard, title banner, and peace decorations.

Build it yourself

How to make the tree

Materials (mostly recycled)

  • Large cardboard box
  • Plastic bottles
  • Old newspapers / magazines
  • Strings, glue, markers, paints
  • Small sticks or rolled cardboard

Steps

  1. Cut and fold the cardboard into the trunk (book style).
  2. Attach the foldable base and slot in the branches.
  3. Add leaves labeled with the 8 values.
  4. Build and attach the spinning wheel, message cylinder, and promise chain.
  5. Add decorations and the title banner.
  6. Test everything for stability.

⏱ About 3–4 hours for a patrol of 6–8 members.

In practice

How to use the tree

During events

  • Show the folded version first.
  • Assemble it quickly in front of the group.
  • Let people spin the wheel, write messages, and tie promises.
  • Run short role-plays of conflict resolution.

In school or community

  • Place it in a peace corner.
  • Students use it whenever a conflict comes up.
  • Patrol members facilitate small dialogue sessions.

When the message cylinder is full

  • Read the messages together, respectfully.
  • Keep good ideas in a "Peace Stories Book."
  • Empty and reuse the cylinder.

Bring it to your patrol

How to replicate this project

Follow the step-by-step guide above. Use any large cardboard box, and involve the whole patrol so everyone learns. Total cost: under KSh 500 if you recycle most materials.
